Eclode is  researching accessible solutions to common problems in muscle genetics. We're using recent advances in gene therapy and genome editing to develop novel, single-use therapies for widespread problems. What if the best Olympic genes in the world were accessible to everyone?

Research

Several genetic targets have shown wide efficacy in improving the mammalian phenotype. One example is myostatin. Myostatin knockout has been successfully performed in mice1, rabbits2, sheep3, goats4, pigs5, dogs6, and monkeys7. In all of these mammals, knocking out myostatin resulted in greatly increased muscle mass. Naturally ocurring myostatin mutants have even been found in humans8. These humans exhibit very lean body composition and greatly increased muscle mass with no known medical complications.

The promise of myostatin knockout models has led to many attempts at inhibiting myostatin to treat diseases such as muscular dystrophy, spinal muscular atrophy, advanced stage cancer, and inclusion body myositis. Strategies for inhibition have included antibodies, proteins, peptides, and genetic modifications. Unfortunately, most of these approaches have failed due to prohibitive costs, difficulty of administration, lack of efficacy, or toxicity complications.

Eclode is researching solutions to problems like these.
